Nigeria is a country located in West Africa, bordered by Benin to the west, Niger to the north, Chad to the northeast, and Cameroon to the east, with the Atlantic Ocean along its southern coast. It is the most populous country in Africa, home to over 200 million people who belong to more than 250 ethnic groups, including the Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo. Nigeria is known for its rich cultural heritage, colorful festivals, and diverse languages. The country has a long and complex history, from ancient kingdoms such as Benin and Oyo to its colonial past under British rule and eventual independence in 1960.
